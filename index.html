About Me

I’m a public health professional and epidemiologist (MPH, PhD) with more than 25 years of experience helping organisations such as the World Health Organization, Food & Agriculture Organization, European Centre for Disease Prevention & Control and Médecins Sans Frontières prepare for and respond to health emergencies around the globe.

My expertise spans health data surveillance‑systems outbreak response One Health and capacity building. I’m passionate about using generative AI and open‑source tools to transform how we analyse data and communicate insights for the public good.

About “Generative AI for Work & Research”

This hands‑on course is part of The GRAPH Network’s mission to make data science & AI accessible for health and life‑science professionals. Over eight weeks we explore:

  • Prompt‑engineering and workflow design for ChatGPT & open‑source LLMs
  • Ethics, bias and governance considerations for AI in public health
  • Integrating AI tools into research pipelines and organisational processes
  • Building lightweight AI‑powered prototypes with Python & R

The programme is beginner‑friendly, career‑focused and supported by universities, NGOs and industry partners to keep tuition affordable.
